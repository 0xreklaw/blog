The Asymptote of Belief

2023 August 24

View All Writing

Since the dawn of time, we have found purpose in the belief of a higher ideal. Religion, wealth, identity; these are all examples of what I call asymptote beliefs. Like an asymptotic line, a belief is something that elevates you to a higher ideal, yet a point that you will never reach. Belief is a perpetual practice that never ends.

As you will notice in the graph, a belief or an ideal is the foundation for your life. As you believe more in an ideal you will elevate your life and find more purpose in it. The more purpose you’ve gained from this belief, the more you will believe in the belief. Each feeding into each other like a perpetual motion machine, you will find purpose out of nothing. As I will go onto explain, there have been three major beliefs through recorded history: religion, wealth, and identity. These three examples are all collective beliefs. A collective belief is a fundamental ideal that is understood by the majority, and it dominates the way a society thinks, feels, operates, and accelerates into the future.

Religion

The first asymptote belief was religion, believe in a higher ideal and you will live a good life. In reality, most people did not live great or humane lives when religion was the most dominant form of belief. This was during times when disease was common, living conditions were despicable, and jobs were long and unforgiving. Religion gave people a why when they shouldn’t of had one. Having a why, defines your existence and pushes you past hardships. Religion was the first example of a collective belief because it gave people a purpose in a purposeless world. If you pray, be a good citizen, then maybe you would reach the higher ideal of morality, heaven. And if you did not, then you would go somewhere much worse than your current reality, hell. As rough as life may have been, people had something to look forward to, reaching heaven, and something to fear, going to hell. Religion kept people ground, in line, and something to base their life on.

Wealth

Wealth was the second asymptotic belief. Developed out of the renaissance and industrial revolution, the crave for money and power propelled humanity forward. As Ricky Bobby says nicely “If you ain’t first, you’re last.” Although blunt, the darwinian statement is a good summary of what capitalism is, a race; if you are not moving up, then you are moving down. Someone will always be working harder than you, and this constant insecurity/fear of falling down in the world hierarchy, gives people a purpose the same way religion does. The ideal of wealth is the belief in money and power, the more of these you gain, the higher up in society you will move. Wealth hit center stage with capitalism which gave people a purpose through the output they produced. At its heights during the late 1800’s and mid to late 1900’s, the belief in wealth slowly transgressed into something else.

Identity

Identity is the forth coming asymptotic belief. As Nietzsche said, “God is Dead. God remains dead. And we have killed him.” Younger generations no longer accept religion, and they are growing wary of the rat race of capitalism. Out of the downfall of both collective beliefs, a third idea has emerged, identity. Post religion and industrialism, more and more people have identified themselves with the LGBTQ+ community. This is the belief and exploration of sex, gender, sexuality and perception oneself. Just like in the Middle Ages, you could never pray enough, in the industrial age you could never have enough power, now you will never fully understand yourself enough. We can see this today with people experiencing gender dysphoria or displeasure with ones orientation with themselves. This displeasure makes individuals look inside and question themselves. Like a female that has changed their gender to a male, they will never be masculine enough, it is therefore their purpose in life to reach the ideal of what a man is.

Thoughts

Belief is a fundamental to society, without it we would not have a purpose in life. Throughout history we have seen the collective belief change, first with religion, then with wealth, and now with identity. All instances are examples of asymptote beliefs because they have no end state. Just like an asymptote, there is no end, you can only get closer and closer, but never find a true end state. In religion you could never pray enough, and if you did believe you could like the papal states of the late Middle Ages did, then your belief was turned to something else wealth. With wealth, you could never work enough, but when people realized had enough, they looked toward something else. Now people are looking into the mirror and finding a purpose through their identity. All beliefs are perpetual unrealized goals, you can never pray enough, you can never work enough, and you will never know yourself enough.
